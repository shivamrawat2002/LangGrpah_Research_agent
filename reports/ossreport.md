## 2. Hiring Practices – From Sourcing to Role Leveling  

### 2.1. Sourcing & Pre‑Screening  

1. **AI‑driven sourcing bots** crawl public profiles, job boards, and internal ATS data, ranking candidates on a **skill‑fit score** that incorporates:  
   - Prompt‑engineering proficiency  
   - Experience with generative‑AI platforms (e.g., Azure OpenAI, Google Gemini)  
   - Prior exposure to AI‑Ops tooling (Kubeflow, MLflow)  

2. **Outcome:** Bots pre‑screen **≈80 %** of inbound applications, automatically rejecting low‑fit profiles and forwarding only high‑potential candidates to human recruiters.  
   - **Speed:** Reduction of manual resume review time from 3 days to < 4 hours.  
   - **Accuracy:** Screening accuracy improves **+25 %** vs. manual processes.  
   - **Source:** [EY podcast (Jan 2026)](https://www.ey.com/en_in/media/podcasts/ai/2026/01/season-2-episode-2-how-agentic-ai-is-redefining-hr-and-workforce-strategies)  

### 2.2. Autonomous Interview Agents  

| Feature | Description | Impact |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| **Technical assessment** | AI agents generate coding challenges or prompt‑engineering tasks, evaluate solutions in real‑time, and score based on correctness, efficiency, and creativity. | Cuts **time‑to‑hire** for AI‑specialist roles from 45 days → **12 days**. |
| **Behavioural fit** | Natural‑language dialogue bots simulate scenario‑based interviews, analysing sentiment, problem‑solving approach, and cultural alignment. | Improves **candidate quality** (25 % higher predictive success). |
| **Decision‑support** | AI aggregates scores, compares against role benchmarks, and recommends hiring tier (associate, senior, lead). | Standardises **role leveling** across geographies. |
